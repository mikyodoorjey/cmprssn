# About

## We exist because the org is the bottleneck.

The technology is ready. Most organizations aren't. Companies keep dropping capable agents into structures designed for humans making decisions at human speed, and the agents inherit the same latency, the same approval queues, the same political seams. The tools work. The org around them doesn't.

We started CMPRSSN because nobody was naming this gap, and the firms that gestured at it priced themselves out of the companies who actually needed the work. Closing the coupling gap is the only problem we work on.

## Why

We believe the org is the binding constraint on intelligence.

Not the model. Not the tooling. Not the talent. The shape of decision rights, the geometry of workflows, the data that does and doesn't flow. That is the ceiling. Lift it, and a company becomes legible to its own agents. Leave it, and every AI investment underperforms its promise.

## How

Three steps, in sequence, never out of order.

**Diagnose.** The Compression Audit. Two weeks, four surfaces, six vectors. We name your binding constraints and deliver the first deposit in your atlas.

**Redesign.** We rebuild the workflows, roles, and decision rules that agents will operate within, not around. That includes the governance: what agents decide, what they escalate, and how every action is logged.

**Deploy.** Through LLABS (production-grade cognitive agents, SOC 2 and HIPAA certified) and Inversed (cryptographic governance for AI: identity, audit trails, compliance), we put agents into a chassis built to hold them.

## What

You leave with five things:

1. A Distortion Declaration that says what is broken and why.
2. A Recompression Roadmap sequenced over 90 days.
3. A Compression Scale score across six vectors.
4. An Agentic Maturity classification.
5. A living atlas. Your organization made legible, in markdown, to the agents that will run inside it.

## The question we open with

Every engagement begins with Jack Dorsey's question, and we hold ourselves to it as much as we hold our clients to it:

> *What does your company understand that is genuinely hard to understand, and is that understanding getting deeper every day?*

Find the deep understanding. Build the infrastructure around it. That is the work.
